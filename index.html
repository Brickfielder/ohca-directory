Heart Attack and Cardiac Arrest - What‚Äôs the Difference?

Both require immediate medical help, but cardiac arrest especially needs instant CPR and defibrillation to restart the heart and save the person's life.

What is the purpose of an OHCA Follow-up and Aftercare directory?

  • Surviving a cardiac arrest is life-changing, and the journey doesn't end once a person leaves the hospital. Many survivors and their families face significant ongoing challenges, including persistent fatigue, memory difficulties, emotional distress, anxiety, or depression.
  • However, finding help can be difficult due to a lack of clear information about available services. The aim of this page is to ensures survivors and their loved ones can find and access the support they need, reducing isolation and improving recovery and quality of life.
